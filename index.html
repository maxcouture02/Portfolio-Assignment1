ABOUT ME

I'm Maxime!
Space Robotics Engineer & Aspiring UI Designer

I’m a bilingual (English/French) computer engineering student from Vaughan, just north of Toronto. Currently, I work on robotics and aerospace innovations, contributing to projects like Canadarm3 and a future lunar vehicle.

Outside of work and school, I love:

  • Experimenting in Unreal Engine 5
  • Tinkering with home servers
  • Building LEGO models
  • Exploring the outdoors

These passions fuel my curiosity and my drive to craft intuitive, engaging interfaces

HOW I WORK

I’m currently enrolled in SEG3125: Analysis and Design of User Interfaces at the University of Ottawa. My go-to resources include our course materials and the Nielsen Norman Group articles and videos.

Hands-On Projects: For my capstone design project, I helped build a rover dashboard using SvelteKit, implementing real-time telemetry from cameras and sensors, interactive rover controls, and the Leaflet maps API. In my real-time embedded-systems course (CEG 4566), I created a security UI in Python Dash, featuring buttons, live-updating graphs, and data panels.

Current Portfolio Design Process: I begin by researching and gathering inspiration from portfolios I admire, then ideate and sketch wireframes. I move into prototyping, and iterate based on usability and insights from my peers.

As the semester progresses, I will continue to edit and add to my portolio, integrating methods learned in class.
